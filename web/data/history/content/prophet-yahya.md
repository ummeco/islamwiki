## Prophet Yahya (John)

Yahya ibn Zakariyya (peace be upon them both) holds a distinguished place among the prophets of Allah. He was born through a miraculous divine gift to elderly parents — his father Zakariyya was advanced in age and his mother was barren — yet Allah willed his existence as a sign of His absolute power over creation. His story is told primarily in Surah Maryam and Surah Aal Imran, where it is linked inseparably to the events surrounding the birth of Isa ibn Maryam.

## A Name Chosen by Allah

Allah Himself named Yahya before his birth, a distinction shared by very few in the Quran. When the angels gave Zakariyya the glad tidings of a son, Allah declared: *"O Zakariyya, indeed We give you good tidings of a boy whose name will be Yahya. We have not assigned to any before this name"* (Surah Maryam 19:7). Ibn Kathir noted that this naming was an act of divine honor, marking Yahya as uniquely chosen from before his entrance into the world. The name itself, derived from the root meaning "to live," carried prophetic weight — Allah granted life where none seemed possible.

## Wisdom from Childhood

Among the most remarkable aspects of Yahya's prophethood is that Allah granted him wisdom and judgement while still a boy. The Quran states: *"O Yahya, take the Scripture with determination. And We gave him judgement while yet a child"* (Surah Maryam 19:12). This was not ordinary intelligence but prophetic discernment — the ability to understand, interpret, and act upon divine revelation at an age when most children are occupied with play.

Ibn Abbas reported that the children once called Yahya to come and play with them, and he replied: "I was not created for play." This narration, cited by numerous classical scholars of tafsir, illustrates the seriousness with which Yahya approached his mission from the earliest age. Al-Hasan al-Basri commented that the distance between Yahya and other children was the distance between purpose and distraction.

## Character and Worship

The Quran praises Yahya with a concentration of virtues rarely gathered for a single figure in a single passage. Allah describes him as possessing *hananan* (tenderness and compassion from Allah), *zakatan* (purity), and *taqiyyan* (God-consciousness and righteousness). He was *barran bi-walidayhi* (dutiful to his parents) and *lam yakun jabbaran asiyyan* (never a disobedient tyrant) (Surah Maryam 19:13–14).

Classical scholars emphasized his extreme asceticism. He wore garments of rough hair, ate locusts and wild plants, and spent his nights in prayer and weeping from the fear of Allah. Al-Tabari recorded that Yahya wept so much that tears left marks on his cheeks. His worship was not performative but came from a deep awareness of standing before Allah, and his abstinence from worldly comforts was total.

## Confirmer of the Word of Allah

Yahya's prophetic mission was directed at the Children of Israel, calling them to repentance and obedience before the coming of Isa ibn Maryam. The Quran records that when Zakariyya received the news of Yahya's birth, he was told his son would be *musaddiqan bi-kalimatin min Allah* — "a confirmer of a word from Allah" (Surah Aal Imran 3:39). The classical mufassirun, including Ibn Kathir and al-Qurtubi, explained that "a word from Allah" refers to Isa, who was created by the divine command "Be" (*kun*). Yahya thus served as a forerunner and witness to the truth of Isa's prophethood, preparing the ground for the message Isa would carry.

The Quran further describes Yahya as *sayyidan* (a leader among his people), *hasuran* (one who restrained himself from desires), and *nabiyyan min al-salihin* (a prophet from among the righteous) (Surah Aal Imran 3:39). The word *hasur* was explained by scholars as indicating his complete mastery over his desires, not out of inability but out of devotion.

## Peace Upon Him

The passage concludes with a verse of extraordinary weight: *"And peace be upon him the day he was born and the day he dies and the day he is raised alive"* (Surah Maryam 19:15). This triple salaam — covering birth, death, and resurrection — is a divine guarantee of security at the three most vulnerable transitions a soul can face. The nearly identical wording appears when Isa speaks about himself (Surah Maryam 19:33), binding these two prophets together in a spiritual kinship that underscores their shared mission in the final prophetic era before Muhammad ﷺ.

## Legacy

Prophet Yahya embodied what it means to live entirely for Allah: wisdom without arrogance, worship without display, authority without tyranny, and compassion without weakness. He remains one of the finest examples of a life devoted to calling people back to their Lord, and his mention in the Quran stands as a reminder that true honor comes not from age, wealth, or worldly power, but from sincerity before Allah and firmness upon His path.
