## Adam: The First Human and First Prophet

Adam (peace be upon him) holds a unique station in Islamic history as both the first human being created by Allah and the first prophet sent to guide humanity. His story, detailed across multiple surahs of the Quran and in authentic hadith, establishes the foundations of human existence, worship, and the relationship between Creator and creation.

## Creation from Clay

Allah created Adam from clay, fashioning him with His own hands and breathing His spirit into him. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "Allah created Adam from a handful of dust taken from all parts of the earth, so the children of Adam came in proportion to the earth. Some are red, some white, some black, and some in between" (Abu Dawud, graded hasan by al-Albani). This narration explains the diversity among Adam's descendants.

Ibn Kathir records in al-Bidayah wan-Nihayah that Adam's body remained as a hollow form of clay for a period before Allah breathed life into him. When the spirit reached his head, he sneezed and said "Alhamdulillah" (All praise is due to Allah), and Allah responded: "Yarhamuka Rabbuk" (May your Lord have mercy on you).

## The Prostration and the Refusal of Iblis

When Allah commanded the angels to prostrate before Adam as an act of honor, all obeyed except Iblis. He refused out of arrogance, claiming superiority: "I am better than him. You created me from fire and created him from clay" (Surah Sad 38:76). This was not a prostration of worship but of respect, and Iblis's refusal became the first act of prideful disobedience. Allah expelled him, and Iblis vowed to mislead Adam's descendants until the Day of Judgment.

## The Knowledge of Names

Allah taught Adam the names of all things, then presented them before the angels and asked them to identify them. When they could not, Adam named them all (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:31-33). This event demonstrated the capacity for knowledge and intellect that Allah granted to humanity, distinguishing them from the rest of creation and justifying Adam's appointment as khalifah on earth.

## Life in the Garden and the Descent

Adam and his wife Hawwa were placed in a garden and given freedom to enjoy all its provisions except one tree. Iblis whispered to them, swearing by Allah that he was a sincere advisor, and they ate from it. Their mistake was not an act of deliberate rebellion but a lapse caused by deception. Immediately they recognized their error and turned to Allah in repentance with the words: "Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves, and if You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers" (Surah Al-A'raf 7:23).

Allah accepted their repentance and taught Adam words of supplication by which to seek forgiveness (Surah Al-Baqarah 2:37). He then sent them down to earth, not as a punishment but as the fulfillment of His original decree to place a vicegerent upon it. Allah promised that guidance would come to them and their descendants, and whoever followed it would have nothing to fear.

## Father of Humanity

On earth, Adam and Hawwa became the parents of all humankind. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "All of you are from Adam, and Adam is from dust" (Tirmidhi). Adam's family grew, and among his children were Habil (Abel) and Qabil (Cain), whose story is told in Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:27-31). When both offered a sacrifice to Allah and Habil's was accepted but Qabil's was not, Qabil killed his brother out of jealousy, committing the first murder in human history.

## Prophethood and Legacy

Adam received revelation from Allah and taught his children tawhid, the worship of Allah alone. Abu Dharr asked the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him): "Was Adam a prophet?" He replied: "Yes, a prophet to whom Allah spoke directly" (Ibn Hibban, sahih). Adam lived for approximately one thousand years, as mentioned in the hadith recorded by al-Tirmidhi regarding the covenant of souls.

The scholars of Ahl us-Sunnah, including Ibn Kathir and al-Tabari, affirm that Adam was sinless in the sense of prophetic integrity. His eating from the tree was a minor lapse, not a persistent sin, and his repentance was accepted completely. Islam rejects the concept of inherited original sin. Every child is born upon the fitrah, the natural disposition toward recognizing Allah.

## Lessons from Adam's Story

Adam's narrative carries several enduring lessons. The mercy of Allah is vast, and sincere repentance is always accepted. Arrogance, as demonstrated by Iblis, is the gravest spiritual disease. Human beings are honored by Allah above much of creation, yet this honor carries the responsibility of obedience and worship. The diversity of humanity traces back to a single origin, establishing the equality of all people before their Creator.

Adam (peace be upon him) laid the foundation upon which every subsequent prophet would build: the call to worship Allah alone, to seek His forgiveness, and to live upon the earth as a trustworthy steward.
