## Dawud (David) in Islam

Dawud (peace be upon him) occupies a unique station among the prophets of Allah. He was granted prophethood, kingship, revealed scripture, and miraculous gifts — yet he remained among the most devoted worshippers to ever walk the earth. His life demonstrates that worldly authority and spiritual devotion are not opposites but can be united in one servant of Allah.

## The Slaying of Jalut

Dawud first rose to prominence during the reign of Talut (Saul), the king appointed by Allah over the Children of Israel. When Talut led his army against the forces of Jalut (Goliath), most of the soldiers faltered. The Quran records that a small, faithful remnant crossed the river with Talut, and from among them, the young Dawud stepped forward to face the giant.

Allah says: *"So they defeated them by the permission of Allah, and Dawud killed Jalut, and Allah gave him the kingdom and prophethood and taught him from that which He willed"* (Al-Baqarah 2:251).

This single act of courage, grounded in trust in Allah rather than in physical strength, became the turning point of Israelite history. Through it, Allah raised Dawud from an ordinary young man to the ruler and prophet of an entire nation.

## The Zabur and the Gift of Song

Allah revealed to Dawud the Zabur (Psalms), a book of divine praise and supplication. The Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) said: *"The Zabur was made easy for Dawud. He would order his riding animals to be saddled, and he would finish reciting the Zabur before they were saddled"* (Sahih al-Bukhari 4713).

More remarkable still was the effect of his voice when he recited. Allah subjected the very mountains and birds to join Dawud in glorifying Him: *"Indeed, We subjected the mountains to glorify with him in the evening and after sunrise, and the birds assembled — all were turning to him in praise"* (Sad 38:18-19). Ibn Kathir notes that when Dawud recited the Zabur, the birds would stop in the sky and echo his praise, and the mountains would respond in kind. This was not metaphor but a real, miraculous subjugation from Allah.

## Iron Made Soft

Among Dawud's unique blessings was mastery over iron. Allah says: *"And We made pliable for him iron, [commanding him]: 'Make full coats of mail and calculate precisely the links'"* (Saba 34:10-11). Iron would become soft in his hands like dough, and he would shape it into coats of chainmail without the need for fire or hammer.

Despite being a king with access to the state treasury, Dawud chose to earn his livelihood through this craft. The Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) said: *"The best food a person eats is that which he earned by his own hands, and the Prophet of Allah, Dawud, used to eat from the work of his own hands"* (Sahih al-Bukhari 2072). This ethic of self-sufficiency while holding the highest office remains a powerful example for leaders.

## The Worship of Dawud

Dawud's devotion to Allah was legendary and became the standard by which voluntary worship is measured. The Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) said: *"The most beloved prayer to Allah is the prayer of Dawud: he used to sleep half the night, pray for a third, and sleep for a sixth. And the most beloved fast to Allah is the fast of Dawud: he used to fast one day and break his fast the next"* (Sahih al-Bukhari 1131).

This pattern — alternating days of fasting and dividing the night between rest and prayer — was praised by the Prophet as the upper limit of what a person should take upon themselves in voluntary worship.

## Wisdom and Judgment

Allah granted Dawud sound judgment and decisive speech. The Quran recounts an episode in which two disputants came to him: one claimed the other's flock of sheep had grazed in his vineyard. Both Dawud and his son Sulayman heard the case, and Allah says: *"And We gave understanding of it to Sulayman, and to each We gave judgment and knowledge"* (Al-Anbiya 21:79). The scholars of tafsir, including al-Tabari, explain that Sulayman proposed a more fitting resolution, and Allah praised both father and son for their wisdom.

## The Trial and Repentance

The Quran alludes to a trial that Dawud faced, after which he turned to Allah in deep repentance: *"And Dawud became certain that We had tested him, so he asked forgiveness of his Lord and fell down bowing and turned in repentance"* (Sad 38:24). The Athari position, following the methodology of the Salaf, avoids speculating about the nature of this trial beyond what the Quran and authentic narrations state. What is clear is that Dawud's response was immediate, sincere repentance, and Allah forgave him and drew him near.

## Legacy

Dawud's reign represents the peak of prophetic governance among the Children of Israel. He united kingship with prophethood, physical strength with spiritual depth, and worldly craft with divine worship. His son Sulayman inherited both his kingdom and his prophetic mission, continuing the golden era his father established.

The Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) held Dawud's worship as the model of balanced devotion, and his example endures as proof that true power lies not in authority over people, but in submission to Allah.
