## Prophet Salih and the People of Thamud

Salih (peace be upon him) was a prophet sent by Allah to the people of Thamud, one of the most prominent ancient Arab civilizations. The Thamud inhabited the region of Al-Hijr in northwestern Arabia, known today as Mada'in Salih, situated between the Hejaz and Greater Syria. Their story is among the most frequently referenced narratives in the Quran, mentioned in over a dozen surahs as a warning to those who reject divine guidance.

## The Civilization of Thamud

The Thamud succeeded the people of 'Ad in inhabiting the Arabian Peninsula and inherited great material prosperity. They were renowned for their architectural skill, carving elaborate homes and palaces directly into the faces of mountains and cliffs. The Quran describes their craft: "And you carve out of the mountains, homes, with skill" (Surah al-Shu'ara 26:149). They possessed fertile gardens, springs, and date palms, living in comfort and security.

Yet despite these blessings, the Thamud fell into the same error as the 'Ad before them. They abandoned the worship of Allah alone and turned to idolatry, while their leaders grew arrogant and oppressive toward the weak among them. The wealthy elite dominated the society, and corruption spread throughout the land.

## The Mission of Salih

Allah raised Salih from among the Thamud themselves. He was a man known to his people for his integrity and wisdom before his prophetic mission. He called them to tawhid with words preserved in the Quran: "O my people, worship Allah; you have no deity other than Him. He has produced you from the earth and settled you in it, so ask forgiveness of Him and then repent to Him. Indeed, my Lord is near and responsive" (Surah Hud 11:61).

Salih reminded his people of the fate of the 'Ad, who had been destroyed for their rejection of Prophet Hud. He urged them to reflect on the blessings Allah had granted them and to recognize that their prosperity was not self-made but a test from their Lord. He warned them not to follow the corrupt leaders who spread mischief: "And do not obey the command of the transgressors, who cause corruption in the land and do not amend" (Surah al-Shu'ara 26:151-152).

The response from the Thamud's elite was dismissal. They accused Salih of being bewitched and questioned why a mere man from among them should be followed. They said: "You are only a man like ourselves. Then bring a sign, if you should be of the truthful" (Surah al-Shu'ara 26:154).

## The She-Camel: A Miraculous Sign

In response to their demand for proof, Allah brought forth a she-camel from within a rock as an unmistakable miracle. This was no ordinary animal but a direct sign from Allah, designated as a test for the Thamud. The camel was to drink from their water on one appointed day, and they would have the water on the alternate day. Salih delivered the warning clearly: "This is the she-camel of Allah, a sign for you. So let her feed upon Allah's earth and do not touch her with harm, lest a painful punishment seize you" (Surah al-A'raf 7:73).

For a time, the arrangement held. But the leaders of Thamud, driven by arrogance and hostility toward the message, conspired to kill the camel. The Quran identifies the perpetrator as "the most wretched of them" (Surah al-Shams 91:12), a man named Qudar ibn Salif according to the classical exegetes including Ibn Kathir and al-Tabari. He hamstrung the she-camel and killed her, an act endorsed by the rest of the community through their silent approval. As Allah states: "Then they hamstrung her and were regretful" (Surah al-Shu'ara 26:157).

## The Destruction of Thamud

After the killing of the she-camel, Salih gave his people a final warning: "Enjoy yourselves in your homes for three days. That is a promise not to be denied" (Surah Hud 11:65). On the fourth day, a devastating blast (al-sayhah) struck the Thamud, and they were annihilated. The Quran describes it: "So the earthquake seized them, and they became within their home fallen prone" (Surah al-A'raf 7:78). Only Salih and those who believed with him were saved by Allah's mercy.

## Legacy and Lessons

The ruins of Thamud endure at Mada'in Salih as a physical testimony to the Quranic account. When the Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) passed through the area during the expedition to Tabuk, he instructed his Companions to weep when passing the ruins and not to drink from the wells there, as recorded in Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim. He said: "Do not enter the dwellings of those who were punished unless you are weeping, lest what befell them befall you."

The story of Salih and Thamud reinforces a central theme of the Quran: material prosperity without obedience to Allah leads to ruin. The Thamud had every worldly advantage, yet their rejection of tawhid and their defiance of a clear divine sign sealed their fate. Their destruction stands as an enduring reminder that no civilization, however powerful, is beyond the reach of Allah's decree.
