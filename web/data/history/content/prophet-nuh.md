## Prophet Nuh in the Quran

Nuh (peace be upon him) holds a distinguished place among the prophets of Allah. He is one of the five Ulul-Azm — the prophets of strong resolve — alongside Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, and Muhammad (peace be upon them all). The Quran refers to him as a grateful servant of Allah (17:3) and dedicates an entire surah to his story (Surah Nuh, 71). He is often called the second father of humanity, as all people alive today descend from those who survived the Great Flood aboard his ark.

## The People of Nuh

Nuh was sent to a people who had fallen into deep polytheism. Their ancestors had been righteous men — Wadd, Suwa, Yaghuth, Ya'uq, and Nasr — whose images were eventually turned into idols after their deaths. Shaytan gradually led the people from honoring the memory of the righteous to worshipping them besides Allah. Ibn Abbas reported that these were originally pious men whose people made statues of them after they died, and over generations, the statues became objects of worship (Sahih al-Bukhari 4920).

This is one of the earliest and clearest lessons in Islamic theology: the path to shirk often begins with seemingly innocent veneration of the dead.

## Nine Hundred and Fifty Years of Da'wah

Allah sent Nuh to call his people back to tawhid — the worship of Allah alone. The Quran states that he remained among them for 950 years (29:14). During this extraordinary span, Nuh employed every method of calling to the truth. He preached openly and privately, by day and by night. He reminded them of Allah's blessings — the rain, the gardens, the rivers, the heavens arranged in layers — urging them to reflect on the signs of their Creator.

Yet his people covered their ears, wrapped themselves in their garments, and persisted in arrogance (71:7). The elite among them dismissed his followers as lowly and insignificant, demanding he drive them away before they would consider listening. Nuh refused, declaring he would never turn away those who sought Allah (11:29-31).

His patience over nearly a millennium stands as one of the most remarkable examples of steadfastness in the history of prophethood.

## The Command to Build the Ark

After centuries of rejection, Allah revealed to Nuh that no more of his people would believe (11:36). He was commanded to build a great ship, far from any sea. The chiefs of his people passed by and mocked him openly, laughing at a prophet building a vessel on dry land. Nuh responded with calm certainty: "If you ridicule us, then we will ridicule you just as you ridicule" (11:38).

He built the ark under divine instruction, preparing for what would become the most decisive act of divine justice the world had witnessed.

## The Great Flood

When Allah's decree came, water burst forth from the earth and poured from the sky. Nuh was commanded to load onto the ark a pair of every species and those who believed with him — a small number after nearly a thousand years of preaching (11:40).

One of the most powerful scenes in the Quran unfolds as the flood begins. Nuh saw his own son standing apart and called out to him: "O my son, come aboard with us and do not be with the disbelievers." His son replied with the confidence of the doomed: "I will take refuge on a mountain to protect me from the water." Nuh cried out, "There is no protector today from the decree of Allah, except for whom He gives mercy." A wave came between them, and his son was among the drowned (11:42-43).

Stricken with grief, Nuh called upon his Lord, reminding Him of His promise to save his family. Allah corrected him firmly: "O Nuh, indeed he is not of your family; indeed, his work was other than righteous" (11:46). This is among the clearest declarations in the Quran that the bond of faith stands above the bond of blood. Lineage and kinship cannot save a person from the consequences of disbelief.

## After the Flood

The waters receded by Allah's command. The earth swallowed the water, the sky cleared, and the ark came to rest upon Mount Judi (11:44). Nuh and the believers disembarked to rebuild human civilization from nothing.

Allah declared: "And We made his descendants the ones who survived. And We left for him favorable mention among later generations. Peace upon Nuh among all peoples" (37:77-79).

## Lessons from the Story of Nuh

The story of Nuh carries lessons that remain urgently relevant. Shirk begins gradually, often disguised as piety. The truth is not determined by majority opinion — the majority rejected Nuh for centuries, yet they were destroyed and the small band of believers inherited the earth. Patience in calling to Allah is not measured in months or years but in a lifetime of unwavering commitment. And no human relationship, however close, can override the distinction between belief and disbelief before Allah.

The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said that on the Day of Judgment, Nuh will be the first messenger questioned, establishing his primacy among the messengers sent to humanity (Sahih Muslim 194).
