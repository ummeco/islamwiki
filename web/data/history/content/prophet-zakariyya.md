## Prophet Zakariyya in Islam

Zakariyya (peace be upon him) was a prophet and priest of the Children of Israel, descended from the line of Sulayman ibn Dawud. He served Allah with devotion throughout a long life and was entrusted with the guardianship of Maryam bint Imran, the mother of Isa. His story in the Quran centers on unwavering faith, heartfelt supplication, and the miraculous gift of a son in extreme old age.

## Lineage and Role

Zakariyya belonged to the prophetic lineage stretching back to Ibrahim through Ishaq and Yaqub. Ibn Kathir records in *al-Bidayah wan-Nihayah* that he was among the priests who served Bayt al-Maqdis (Jerusalem) and was chosen by divine lot to be the guardian of Maryam when her mother dedicated her to the service of the temple. The Quran states: "And He made Zakariyya her guardian" (Aal Imran 3:37).

His role placed him in direct contact with one of the most blessed women in human history, and it was through observing the signs of Allah's provision for Maryam that his own story took its remarkable turn.

## The Miracle of Maryam's Provisions

Whenever Zakariyya entered the prayer chamber where Maryam worshipped, he found fresh, out-of-season provisions with her. When he asked about their source, she replied: "It is from Allah. Indeed, Allah provides for whom He wills without account" (Aal Imran 3:37).

This encounter struck Zakariyya deeply. If Allah could provide sustenance from the unseen for this young woman, could He not also grant a child to an aging prophet whose wife was barren? It was at that very moment, as the Quran records, that Zakariyya called upon his Lord.

## The Supplication

The du'a of Zakariyya in Surah Maryam is among the most moving passages in the Quran. He called upon Allah in secret, saying:

> "My Lord, indeed my bones have weakened, and my head has filled with white, and never have I been in my supplication to You, my Lord, unhappy. And indeed, I fear the successors after me, and my wife has been barren, so give me from Yourself an heir who will inherit me and inherit from the family of Yaqub. And make him, my Lord, pleasing to You." (Maryam 19:4-6)

His concern was not worldly inheritance but the continuation of prophetic guidance among his people. He feared that those who would lead after him lacked righteousness, and he sought a son who would carry forward the message of tawhid and obedience to Allah.

In Surah al-Anbiya, his supplication is summarized with equal tenderness: "My Lord, do not leave me alone, and You are the best of inheritors" (al-Anbiya 21:89).

## The Divine Response

Allah answered Zakariyya's prayer and sent the angels with glad tidings: "O Zakariyya, indeed We give you good tidings of a boy whose name will be Yahya. We have not assigned to any before this name" (Maryam 19:7). The name Yahya was itself a divine gift, unprecedented among his people.

Astonished despite his faith, Zakariyya asked how this could be when he had reached extreme old age and his wife was barren. The response came: "It is easy for Me, as I created you before when you were nothing" (Maryam 19:9). This echoes a recurring Quranic principle: Allah's power is not constrained by material causes.

As a sign confirming the miracle, Zakariyya was told he would not speak to people for three days, communicating only through gestures (Aal Imran 3:41). During this period he glorified Allah morning and evening, and the sign served to mark the beginning of the fulfilled promise.

## His Character and Rank

The Quran praises Zakariyya alongside other prophets in Surah al-Anbiya: "And Zakariyya, when he called to his Lord, 'My Lord, do not leave me alone, and You are the best of inheritors.' So We responded to him, and We gave to him Yahya, and amended for him his wife. Indeed, they used to hasten to good deeds and supplicate Us in hope and fear, and they were to Us humbly submissive" (al-Anbiya 21:89-90).

Ibn Kathir notes that "amended for him his wife" means Allah cured her barrenness, enabling her to conceive. The verse highlights three qualities of this prophetic family: racing toward good deeds, supplicating with both hope and fear, and deep humility before Allah.

## His Death

Islamic tradition records that Zakariyya was martyred by the corrupt among the Children of Israel. Several narrations collected by Ibn Kathir and others describe that he was killed while hiding inside a tree, which was sawed in half with him inside it. Though the chain of these reports varies in strength, this account is widely accepted among classical scholars and reflects the broader Quranic theme that the Children of Israel killed their prophets unjustly (al-Baqarah 2:61).

## Lessons from Zakariyya's Story

Zakariyya's life teaches that no supplication is too great for Allah to answer. A man of advanced age with a barren wife received a son by divine decree, because he turned to Allah with sincerity, humility, and persistence. His story affirms that the means of this world do not limit the power of the Creator, and that those who call upon Allah with certainty will never be left disappointed.
