# The Prophet's Last Sermon in the Mosque

During his final illness, the Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) gave several addresses to his companions from the mosque in Medina and from Aisha's chamber. These final sermons represent the Prophet's own summary of his spiritual and moral priorities at the end of his life, his concern for the community he was leaving, and his farewell to the companions who had followed him for over two decades.

## Context

The Prophet's final illness lasted approximately two weeks before his death on 12 Rabi al-Awwal, 11 AH. During this period there were intervals when his condition improved enough for him to address the community. He spoke at the mosque on more than one occasion; scholars of the seerah have identified several distinct addresses from this period.

## Content of the Final Mosque Address

The Prophet addressed the congregation on what appears to have been one of his last appearances at the mosque while still able to walk (or be carried) there. Key themes included:

**The warning against glorifying graves.** The Prophet said: "Allah cursed the Jews and Christians, for they took the graves of their prophets as places of worship." Aisha reported that he said this as a warning to his community, fearing they might do the same after his death. This hadith became foundational for the Islamic position on the permissibility of visiting graves versus directing worship toward them.

**Spiritual equality in prayer.** The Prophet addressed the community as a unified body, reminding them that no prayer is accepted except with presence of heart and consciousness of Allah.

**Concern for the Ansar.** In his final addresses, the Prophet spoke specifically about the Ansar — the Muslims of Medina who had hosted the community: "Take care of the Ansar, for the people will increase and the Ansar will diminish. They are my inner garment (shi'ar) while people are my outer garment (dithar). Treat their good-doers well and forgive their transgressors."

**The rights of those in one's charge.** He repeatedly said: "Prayer! Prayer! And what your right hands possess" — meaning: maintain the prayer and care for those under your responsibility. This was among his most frequently repeated warnings during the illness.

**Freeing of slaves.** The Prophet urged the freeing of slaves and the fulfillment of obligations to those in one's household. The hadith transmitted by Aisha and Ali records this as one of the Prophet's consistent refrains as he weakened.

## The Address from the Pulpit

In one narration, the Prophet mounted the minbar (pulpit) for the last time during his illness, delivering an address to the assembled community. He spoke of Abu Bakr and referenced the Cave (of Thawr during the hijra), indicating Abu Bakr's unique closeness to him. He also spoke of the virtue of the mosque itself.

According to some narrations, on this occasion he formally closed off all doors that opened into the mosque except Abu Bakr's door — a detail that, like the prayer appointment, was later understood as signaling Abu Bakr's special status.

## Farewell Character

The companions who heard these final addresses recognized — at least in retrospect — that the Prophet was saying farewell. He spoke of his imminent departure, of the community's responsibilities after him, and of the Two Things he was leaving them (the Quran and his Sunnah) to guide them.

Ibn Masud reported that after the Prophet's final illness became apparent, Abu Bakr wept. The Prophet said to him: "Do not weep." This exchange underscored the personal weight of the final days for the companions closest to the Prophet.

## Sources

The Prophet's final addresses in the mosque are recorded across multiple hadith collections. Key narrators are Aisha bint Abi Bakr, Anas ibn Malik, Abdullah ibn Abbas, and Abu Hurayrah. Primary sources: Sahih Bukhari (Kitab al-Marda, Kitab al-Maghazi), Sahih Muslim, the Muwatta of Imam Malik, and the biographical works of Ibn Hisham and Ibn Saad (*al-Tabaqat al-Kubra*).
