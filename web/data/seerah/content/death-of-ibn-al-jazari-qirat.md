# Death of Ibn al-Jazari: Master of Quranic Recitation

Shams ad-Din Abu al-Khayr Muhammad ibn Muhammad ibn Muhammad ibn Ali ibn Yusuf al-Jazari (751-833 AH / 1350-1429 CE) was the foremost authority on Quranic recitation (tajwid and qira'at) of the medieval period. His works systematized the science of the ten canonical recitations of the Quran and established standards for the teaching of Quranic recitation that remain authoritative to this day.

## Life

Ibn al-Jazari was born in Damascus in 751 AH (1350 CE). He began his study of the Quran at an early age, memorizing it and then pursuing advanced study of the ten recognized canonical recitations (al-qira'at al-ashr) under the leading masters of his time. He traveled extensively across the Islamic world to study under different scholars of recitation — to Egypt, Mecca, and various Syrian cities.

He held positions of religious leadership in Damascus, including as director of the Dar al-Hadith al-Ashrafiyya and as prayer leader at the Umayyad Mosque. He was captured along with other scholars when Timur (Tamerlane) sacked Damascus in 803 AH (1401 CE) and taken to Timur's court, where he reportedly impressed the conqueror with his learning and recitation.

After Timur's death, Ibn al-Jazari eventually made his way to Anatolia and then to Shiraz in Persia, where he spent his later years under Timurid patronage. He died in Shiraz in 833 AH (1429 CE) at approximately 82 years of age.

## The Science of Qira'at

The ten canonical recitations of the Quran represent legitimate variations in the vocalization, letter forms, and some words of the Quranic text — variations traceable through chains of transmission to Companions of the Prophet or to the Prophet himself. The Companion Uthman's standardization of the written text (mushafs) preserved a consonantal skeleton (rasm) that was compatible with multiple recitation traditions.

The ten canonical readings were systematized by scholars over several centuries. Ibn Mujahid (d. 324 AH / 936 CE) famously standardized seven readings; three additional readings were later accepted, making ten.

## Major Works

**Al-Nashr fil-Qira'at al-Ashr** (The Spreading of the Ten Recitations). Ibn al-Jazari's masterwork is a comprehensive two-volume encyclopedia covering the ten canonical recitations in full detail. For each recitation, it explains the principles, the chains of transmission, the specific rules, and the differences from the standard Hafs an Asim recitation used across most of the Muslim world. It is the fundamental reference for advanced students of Quranic recitation.

**Al-Tayyiba** (also called *Tayyibat al-Nashr*). A versified summary of *al-Nashr*, presenting the rules of the ten recitations in a poem of approximately 1000 verses to facilitate memorization. Like the *Shatibiyya* before it, the *Tayyiba* became a standard memorization text for students of the ten recitations.

**Al-Muqaddimah al-Jazariyya** (The Jazari Introduction). A short poem on the fundamentals of tajwid (the rules of proper Quranic recitation), covering the articulation points of letters (makhraj), their characteristics, and the basic rules. It is the most widely memorized tajwid text in the Islamic world and is typically among the first things a Quran student memorizes after the Quran itself.

**Ghayat al-Nihaya fi Tabaqat al-Qurra** (The Ultimate Limit: Biographical Dictionary of Quran Reciters). A biographical dictionary of approximately 3,800 scholars of Quranic recitation, providing an indispensable record of the transmission chains of Quranic recitation through Islamic history.

## Legacy

Ibn al-Jazari's systematization of the ten recitations and his pedagogical texts established a standard that has been maintained in institutions of Quranic education across the Muslim world for nearly six centuries. Students who complete advanced study of Quranic recitation receive ijazas (licenses) tracing their chain of transmission back through their teachers, ultimately connecting to Ibn al-Jazari and through him to the early masters of the recitation traditions.

His *Muqaddimah* is recited and memorized by students of the Quran from Morocco to Indonesia, making it one of the most widely known texts in the Islamic world alongside the Quran itself.
